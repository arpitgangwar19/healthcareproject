Obesity – important facts, causes, and treatment

The term "obese" refers to someone who has too much bodily fat.

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), being overweight or obese causes at least 2.8 million adult deaths annually. Additionally, being overweight and obese contributes to 44% of diabetes cases, 23% of heart disease cases, and 7-41% of certain cancer cases. Over 30 million Indians are obese, and the figure is frighteningly rising.

It is not only a matter of appearance. Obesity is a medical issue that raises your risk ofdeveloping various illnesses and health issues, including heart disease, diabetes, high blood pressure, and several malignancies. Living with obesity and being overweight can reduce your quality of life, worsen mental health issues like depression, and lower your self-esteem.


Body mass index (BMI) is often used to diagnose obesity.

A person's BMI can help determine whether or not they are at a healthy weight.

Important facts and causes of obesity

  • Obesity is primarily brought about by an energy imbalance between calories consumed and calories burned.

  • It is not merely a result of a lack of control over eating habits. Obesity may also result from uncontrollable genetic, behavioural, hormonal, and metabolic factors.

  • Additionally, some underlying medical disorders, such as hypothyroidism, an underactive thyroid, and others, can cause weight gain.

  • Some medications, such as steroids, can increase a person's likelihood of gaining weight.

  • Excessive weight gain during infancy also raises the risk of adult obesity.

  • Smoking during pregnancy and being overweight increase the likelihood of giving birth to a child who is more likely to grow up to be an obese adult.

  • The biggest contributor involves the Western diet: frequent, large meals loaded with refined grains, red meat, unhealthy fats, and sugary drinks.

  • Cortisol levels rise as a result of ongoing stress, which also causes weight gain. Cortisol and other stress hormones can intensify "carb cravings" and make it challenging to usesound judgment and willpower, which can lead to bad eating decisions.

  • Lower physical activity and a lack of sleep are also important emerging risk factors for obesity.

  • In most countries, obesity cause more fatalities than starvation.
